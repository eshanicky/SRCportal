Women in Research (WIRe)

Gender equality in science is not merely a question of fairness. To strengthen research, total human capital must be utilised. A better gender balance will mean that the universities, colleges and research institutes will secure the best talents from both sexes, as well as reflecting population diversity. We aspire to become a truly knowledge-based society. For a knowledge-based society, society traditions (including traditional gender roles) need to be challenged.To not fully use the potential of all qualified people will affect quantity and especially quality of scientific research. Having gender balance in research brings science closer to society by reflecting the actual composition of society. Thus science can be more relevant to society’s needs.

At SRC, we aim at :

  • 1. Inviting groups which conduct talks and presentation across the globe to promote women in research. This will give international exposure to the participants of SRC.
  • 2. Conducting presentation and talks by influential women who have made a successful career in research in the Students’ Research Convention and inspire the community.
  • 3. Spreading awareness about the life in Academia and introducing research as a viable career option by conducting sessions in the various halls of residence.
